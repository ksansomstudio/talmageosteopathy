What is Osteopathy?

Founded by Dr. Andrew Taylor Still in the late 19th century, osteopathy emerged as a holistic approach to healthcare. Dr. Still, a physician disillusioned with conventional medicine's reliance on drugs and surgery, developed osteopathy based on the principle that the body has the inherent ability to heal itself. He believed that structural imbalances and restrictions in the musculoskeletal system could disrupt the body's natural healing processes. As classically trained Osteopathic Manual Practitioners, our treatments focus on gentle manual manipulations to restore mobility, alleviate pain, and promote overall wellness.

What can Osteopathy help with?

Osteopathy aims to improve the health and function of the entire body. Some benefits of osteopathic treatment include:
1. Decreased acute or chronic pain: Osteopathic manipulative techniques can help alleviate pain by addressing musculoskeletal imbalances and dysfunctions.
2. Improved mobility: Osteopathic treatments can enhance joint mobility and flexibility, allowing for better movement and function. 3. Improved immune function: By promoting proper circulation and lymphatic drainage, osteopathy can support immune system function.
4. Improved cardiopulmonary health: Osteopathic techniques can aid in improving cardiovascular and respiratory function, contributing to overall heart and lung health.
5. Improved digestion: Osteopathic manipulation may help alleviate digestive issues by addressing any restrictions or dysfunctions in the gastrointestinal tract.
6. Decreased swelling or inflammation: Osteopathic manipulation can help reduce swelling and inflammation by improving circulation and lymphatic drainage.
7. Reduced stress on the body: By addressing musculoskeletal imbalances and promoting relaxation, osteopathy can help reduce overall stress and tension in the body.
8. Improved sleep: Osteopathic treatments can help promote relaxation and relieve tension, leading to better sleep quality.
9. Improved function of all body systems: Osteopathy aims to restore balance and function to all systems of the body, including musculoskeletal, cardiovascular, respiratory, nervous, endocrine, and reproductive systems.
Many individuals choose to incorporate osteopathic treatment into their regular healthcare routine as a preventive measure to maintain optimal health and function. Just as people engage in regular exercise, eat nutritious foods, and practice stress management techniques to support their health, they may also seek osteopathic care to ensure their body's structural integrity and function are maintained.
